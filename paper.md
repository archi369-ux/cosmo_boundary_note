# Boundary Semantics & Record-Preserving Cosmology (Concept Paper)

## Abstract
We develop an exploratory, theory-first framework in which cosmological “breakdowns” (e.g., singular limits reached by extrapolating smooth-spacetime General Relativity) are interpreted as **domain/meaning boundaries** rather than literal arithmetic failures. citeturn1search3turn1search10 The framework’s central commitment is a **record-preserving ontology**: a total record structure (T) is assumed real and immutable, while time (t) is treated as a traversal index through T. Under this assumption, earlier relational information remains recoverable via rewind of t, shifting ‘information loss’ from expansion-driven inaccessibility to explicit record corruption/erasure rules. We further separate a 3D coordinate space model used for pattern description from dark energy, which remains operationally defined by its role in late-time accelerated expansion. citeturn1search12turn1search10

## 1. Observational anchors
- Age of the observable universe ~13.8 Gyr (Planck 2018 constraints). citeturn1search1turn1search3
- Accelerated expansion as baseline; dark energy defined operationally. citeturn1search12turn1search10

## 2. Boundary semantics
Singular behavior is treated as a signal that the smooth-spacetime model is being used beyond its regime of meaning; boundaries therefore mark where questions become undefined in the language. citeturn1search3turn1search10

## 3. Record-preserving ontology
Assume a total record structure T that is immutable unless explicitly altered. Time t is traversal through T; rewind of t recovers earlier records.

## 4. Space model and ‘center’ language
We use a 3D coordinate chart (x,y,z) with an origin O as a reference convention. Standard cosmology does not require a unique spatial center for the Big Bang, so any ‘center’ usage here is treated as coordinate convention rather than physical privilege. citeturn1search5turn1search10

## 5. Dark energy separation
Dark energy is not equated with space; it is defined by its effect on accelerated expansion. citeturn1search12turn1search10

## 6. Outlook
Next steps define the substrate of T in 3D patterns and formalize end-states as cessation of new records vs record corruption.
